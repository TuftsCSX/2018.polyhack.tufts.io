First Time Hacker Cheat Sheet


TL;DR: Bring a friend and plan to stay the night



Hack? Is this legal?
  • "Hacking" has a bad rep in the media, but here we're talking about making something cool in a short amount of time with limited resources (think "life hack")

Gear:
  • A friend (preferably at a similar skill level)
  • Toothbrush, computer, charger, comfortable clothes, your brain
  • Optional: extension cord, dry erase marker, sticky notes, pen + paper (we will have some at the event, but may not be able to give to everyone), change of clothes

Designer vs Hacker?
  • It usually takes longer to build the tech than to design, so we will also have design competitions and events at night to keep designers on their toes
  • The best designer will have a 1:1 portfolio review with a Facebook desinger. The top five designs will be submitted to Facebook for them to pick a winner
  • You can be a hacker or designer and still submit to the design contests
  • All design competition submissions must be designed at Tufts Polyhack

Schedule:
    Coming soon!

What kind of projects can I make?
  • Anything! Everything from a website that tells your grandma to get off the computer to your own programming language
  • A lot of the best projects are either really serious and impressive, really creative, or really funny jokes #trolls

How do prizes work?
  • First Place and Runner Up: decided by the organizers + sponsors
  • Sponsor Specific Awards: best use case of their API
  • People's Choice: We will have a voting system after the event for people to choose the project they liked the most but that didn't get an award
  • Accolades: the organizers have picked a handful of badges for outstanding projects
  • Designers: There are prizes throughout the night specifically for you guys

  • WE DO NOT READ YOUR RESUME
  • They are strictly for the sponsors, who want to give you jobs

Polyhack is a joint project between the Tufts Computer Science Exchange and Tufts.io
